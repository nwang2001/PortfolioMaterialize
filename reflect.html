Reflections

Professional Development

Throughout the Gateway program, I've came to the realization that it has parallels to what I learned in my previous business and JROTC classes. It became evident that the foundational skills I acquired in those classes, such as presentation skills and proper communication etiquette, became the bedrock that Gateway built upon. Another realization I made during the Gateway experience was the immense importance of self-discipline and time management. The program encouraged us to take charge of our own learning and development, and it became clear that our success depended heavily on our ability to manage our time effectively and stay focused on our goals. Attending the Blacks in Tech career fair and stopping at the Road to Hire table allowed me to gain a deeper understanding of the program's structure. I realized Road to Hire and the Gateway program is not just about us acquiring technical skills, but it also places emphasis on personal growth and professional development. I believe this program will be a remarkable opportunity for personal and professional growth.


Technical

Before the Gateway process started, I was advised to brush up on my HTML and CSS. This preparation proved beneficial as I tackled the HTML and CSS assignments. It became evident that I possessed the necessary skills, but to truly excel and become more efficient, I needed to devote more time to practice. While watching the video tutorials provided a foundation, I understood that deep understanding of the subjects taught required active engagement. I had to use additional resources to conduct further research and experiment on my own to ensure I comprehend the concepts correctly. One feature of web developement I struggled to learn is media queries. I didn't understand its usage and when to use it. After learning about it in Gateway and conducting further research, I was able to grasp a better understanding and use it in this project. I like that at the end of his videos, Mr. McNeil will have his slide that says "practice makes progress" and I agree with it. As technology evolves, it's beneficial to have a growth mindset and stay open to continuous learning. Through consistent practice, I can make tangible progress in my technical skills.


Personal

I realized that I still have the same fascination with coding as I did in high school. The world of coding is vast and complex, with numerous coding languages to explore. While it can be frustrating at times, the sense of accomplishment when the code works is very rewarding. I enjoy making websites more appealing and visually engaging. To me, the process of transforming a blank code page into a functional and aesthetically pleasing website is incredibly fulfilling. I also appreciate the opportunity to learn new coding elements and later applying them to my projects. As I reflect on my Gateway experience, I am grateful for the opportunity to learn more about coding and web development.
